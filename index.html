  • Chuck

    The Chuck is located in the shoulder section of the cow, precisely the lower neck and the upper shoulder of it, besides this the Chuck would taste good for every style of cooking you do to it.

  • Brisket

    The Brisket is located from the breast/pectoral area of the cow, the brisket is also one of the primal cuts on beef, keep in mind to also be careful when you are slow cooking, the brisket, since if you cook it wrongly it could effect its taste and juicy flavour

  • Sirloin

    The Sirloin comes from the top of the cow's back, this is also know as leaner cuts, the best way to cook a sirloin is to "Grill, skillet and stir fry with high dry heats" according to finedininglovers.com

  • Plate

    The plate or the sternum area, is known to be the extension of the rib primal, this would also be served best if the plate cut is grilled or fried, since the flavors in the plate are very rich

  • Rib

    The Ribs are located between the chuck and sirloin/loin, it is also very commen for people too bake the ribs, as they give the best flavor

  • Loin

    They are located in the same spot as where the Sirloins are, they are located in the rear of the animal or the back of the animal, and to get the best flavour, you should grill or fry them

  • Flank

    The Flank is located or is in the section of the sirloin/loin, precisely in the stomach, and they are best to be grilled or fried, to get the best flavour out of them.

  • Round

    Located in the rear or back leg of the cow, to get the best flavour out of the "round", you should roast it

Beef Grading

What is it? and How can it be Done?

  • Beef Grading

    Let us first talk about how it works, first of all, we need to understand that beef grading only works for Wagyu.

    Wagyu is a type of beef that is expected to be of, exceptional quality, appearance, and flavour, that is why they added wagyu grading, it gives people a better idea if the wagyu that they are buying, some of the examples that are to be considered in the grading of wagyu beef is its, meat colour, its fat, and marbling.

    In the Japanese Beef Grading system, there is a grade that is given to the wagyu, it is graded from 1 to 5, from 1 being the poorest to 5 being the highest or the most excellent quality there is.

    • History of Wagyu in the US

      Let us first talk about how it works, first of all, we need to understand that beef grading only works for Wagyu.

      Wagyu is a type of beef that is expected to be of, exceptional quality, appearance, and flavour, that is why they added wagyu grading, it gives people a better idea if the wagyu that they are buying, some of the examples that are to be considered in the grading of wagyu beef is its, meat colour, its fat, and marbling.

      • Now Let us Get into the History of Wagyu

        The first wagyu cattle that was brought to US from Japan was in the Year 1976, during their first transport, only 4 bulls made the long journey from Japan to the US, there was no more wagyu cattle transportation in the US until the 1990’s

        But then, in 1997 Japan had decided to ban exports to other countries, since they decided that Wagyu is a living national treasure. So, between the first cattle that was transported to US in 1976 and the export ban in 1997, there was only 200 wagyu that was successfully exported to the US

        Since Japan banned exports of their Wagyu, the US decided to make their own breed of wagyu. The Americans, decided to crossbreed, but due to the crossbreeding that they have done, fewer than 5000 are 100% Fullblood Wagyu

        • Fullblood wagyu is a Wagyu is defined as Wagyu cattle that maintains an unadulterated bloodline, with absolutely no crossbreeding in its genetics, and is directly traceable back to Japanese ancestry. The rest are what are known as “percentage” Wagyu

          For a beef to be called wagyu, there are a few guidelines. In order a wagyu to be called a fullblood wagyu, it must not only be 100% Wagyu, but it must also be genetically certified to not have any crossbreeding in its lineage

          • Although a cattle with as little as 50% Wagyu can be called wagyu, this is also the most common type of wagyu in the United states as well. But there are also higher grades as well, there is also the 75% Wagyu, known as F2, there is also Purebred Wagyu which is, 94.75% Wagyu genetics. The higher the percentage, the more prevalent the inherent qualities of Wagyu are, such as intense marbling, which gives the beef the tenderness and flavour its famed for

            So, this is a bit of the background of the wagyu grading system and wagyu itself in the US
